Ready, steady, COOK!

  • INGREDIENTS

    • 2 kg red peppers
    • 1 kg carrots
    • 2 kg zucchini
    • 1 kg onions
    • 1 kg tomatoes
    • 0,7 kg sausage
    • 1 l tomato passata
    • salt, pepper, paprika powder, basil, oregano
    • 2 tbs butter

  • Carrots

    Dice vegetables, starting with carrots, as they are the hardest and they need to be cooked the longest. Place carrots in a big pot, add water to cover them and cook on medium heat. Dice red peppers and fry them on hot oil, so that the peppers taste got deeper. Add sweet paprika powder and fry for 1 more minute. After that place red peppers in the pot.

  • Other vegetables

    Dice zucchini and add to the pot. Diced onions saute in the oil and mix with the rest of the vegetables. Dice and add tomatoes (peel them first). Add tomato sauce and seasonings: salt, pepper, basil and oregano.

  • Sausages

    Dice Polish sausage and fry on the pan. Mix with vegetables. You can easily have vegetarian version of Polish Leczo, if you will skip this step. Vegetarian Leczo is also a great choice for Summer.

  • Coock 40 minutes

    Cook for another 40 minutes. Once carrots are ready, you can turn off the heat.

  • Butter

    At the end add 2 tablespoons of butter, I’m telling you, this is a magic ingredient of Polish Leczo

  • Storage

    In order to save the Summer tastes of Leczo for Autumn, you can preserve it in mason jars. Fill jars with -this is important!-hot Leczo, clean jar’s edge and cover with lid. Put the jars up side down and leave overnight for a slow cool down. The next day check the lid, it should be sucked down. Store in a fridge up to 2 months. You can also freeze Leczo.

  • How to serve

    Serve with good rye bread and butter or with rice. Smacznego
